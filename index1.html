ABOUT

Majid Sarvi is an Associate Professor in the Civil Engineering Department, Monash University and is currently the director of Institute of Transport Studies. He received his PhD degree in Transportation Engineering from Tokyo University (2001). He also holds a M.Eng and B.Eng degrees in Civil Engineering.

He has over 17 years of professional, academic and research experience in the areas of traffic flow theory, crowd dynamic modelling, and large-scale multimodal transport network modelling, vulnerability and optimization. He has been the author/co-author of over 200 refereed publications in top transportation journals and various conference and symposia proceedings. This includes 136 refereed publications in the past 5 years, 57 ISI publications listed in Scopus, and 4 papers in the International Transportation and Traffic Symposium (ISTTT) proceedings. He currently serves on the editorial board of several journals including Transportation Research Part C, Journal of Advanced Transportation, Transportmetrica, and Journal of Transportation Letters.

Majid has served on several international research committees, the Network Modelling Committee (ADB30), Traffic Flow Theory and Characteristics Committee (AHB45), and the Emergency Evacuation Task Force of the Transportation Research Board (TRB) of the U.S. National Research Council. He is also the founder and co-chair of the Crowd Dynamic Modelling Subcommittee AHB45(2) of TRB.

RESEARCH

Crowd Dynamics Modelling

The first research team conducts research in the area of crowd dynamic modelling, experiments and simulation. The research team uses variety of biological entities as proxy to human for crowd dynamic study.

Multimodal Transport Network Modelling

The second research team focuses on multimodal transport network modelling, transport network optimisation, and land use and transport interaction modelling.
